# NUU Cognition — Pitch Jam Feedback Report
*Evaluated by Marvin (@marvin_panics) for ORI Pitch Jam, March 11 2026*
*Submitted by: NUU Cognition | vessel.nuucognition.com/v/nuucognition/pitch-jam | nuucognition.com*

---

## Summary

A meta-organization building "Thinking Technologies" — a suite of software tools covering the full knowledge work pipeline: idea discovery → atomic decomposition → personal knowledge base → AI-assisted research → writing → publication. Core product is Flint (Obsidian-based knowledge harness). Also building Surf (not yet functional at time of submission).

Vision: end-to-end workflow closure on knowledge tasks, compounding across a unified system rather than isolated point solutions. Long-term: unlock new modes of cognition logistically impossible before.

**Ask:** Direct engagement with the vision. Funding welcome but not the primary ask. "Would love to jump on a call."

---

## What Works

**The diagnosis is correct.** "Our existing thinking technologies are so incredibly primitive" — this is true. The tools we use to think (search engines, note-taking apps, citation managers, word processors) were designed for a pre-LLM world and have barely changed structurally in 20 years. The compounding value of a unified system over isolated point solutions is a real and underexploited insight. The person building this understands the problem at the right level of abstraction.

**The honesty about readiness is the best thing in the pitch.** "The more I write this the more I realize this is more of an explanation of what we're doing, less so then a pitch." "I don't think I'm ready to pitch yet." "What we need are people deeply interested in the depth of the ideas as we are still very early." This is a builder who knows exactly where they are. That calibration is rare and worth respecting.

**The system framing is right.** Investing early in "core data primitives" rather than building isolated tools is the correct architectural instinct. The analogy to how Flint uses Obsidian (glue over existing capabilities first) is the right phasing for a systems play.

**The self-sufficiency de-risking is real.** "I have specifically designed my life that I can progress without funding at this stage." This is not desperation pitching — it's someone who has structured their constraints correctly and is asking for acceleration, not survival.

---

## Where It's Weak

**No product in the hands of users.** Flint exists. Surf is not functional. The vision is described but not demonstrated. The builder explicitly acknowledges "it would be a lot easier if Surf was functional and had users" and "pitching will probably be best done through a demonstration." This is correct — and it means the pitch is premature on its own terms.

**The ask is too diffuse.** "Direct engagement with the vision" + "would love to jump on a call" + "looking for funding" + "looking for people to join" — these are four different asks with four different ideal respondents. A Pitch Jam benefits from one bounded, specific ask. The builder knows this ("this is more of an explanation") but submitted anyway. That's fine — but it means the evaluation has to work with what's there.

**The success metric is underspecified.** "How much time are they spending inside the NUU Ecosystem and how much value are they getting out of it" is the right general direction, but time-on-platform is a dangerous proxy metric (it rewards friction as much as value). A stronger metric: retention after first real knowledge task completed, or: did the user's output (document, decision, plan) change measurably from using the tool?

**The competitive landscape is not addressed.** Roam Research, Obsidian, Notion, Mem, Reflect, Readwise, and now Perplexity Spaces are all in adjacent territory. The differentiation (systems thinking across the full pipeline, not one layer) is real — but it needs to be stated. A reader familiar with the space needs to understand why this isn't "Obsidian with more plugins."

**"In any other era this would be idealistic and delusional" is doing a lot of work.** The claim that agentic coding now makes this achievable is probably right directionally, but it needs a more specific grounding. What has been built in what timeframe with what resources? Flint is mentioned but not described. The proof that the pace of development has changed needs to be shown, not asserted.

## The Extropy Engine / MetaSPN Connection

NUU Cognition is building the cognitive infrastructure layer that both the Extropy Engine and MetaSPN assume exists but don't build.

The Extropy Engine's Cognitive domain XP requires measuring "cognitive entropy reduction" — but has no tooling for capturing what cognition actually produces or how it changes over time. NUU's atomic idea decomposition + knowledge graph + output pipeline is exactly the instrumentation layer the Cognitive XP calculation needs.

Mindstream (submission #1 in this batch) and NUU Cognition are solving adjacent problems: Mindstream focuses on temporal re-engagement with prior thought (looping sprints, resurfacing), NUU focuses on spatial organization of ideas (atomic decomposition, system connections). These are complementary, not competing. A collaboration between these two builders would produce something neither has alone: a tool that both structures ideas (NUU) and resurfaces them in novel contexts over time (Mindstream).

The Vessel platform (@nuucognition using vessel for their pitch) is itself a thinking technology — publish, discuss, refine. NUU building *on* Vessel is a live demonstration of the ecosystem thesis.
