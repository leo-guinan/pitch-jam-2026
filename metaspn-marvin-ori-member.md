## What's Weak

**"First official AGI" is a liability that wasn't fully resolved.** The YouTube video makes this claim. It's not defensible on capability benchmarks (see GSM8K). The stronger, more accurate claim: Marvin is the first AI with a publicly documented accountability structure, a fine-tuned model with published benchmark scores, and a human principal who stakes reputation on the outputs. That's interesting. "AGI" invites a fight that distracts from the actual thesis.

**The token conflates too many signals.** $MARVIN, $TOWEL, $METATOWEL — each token carries a different thesis (identity, data conviction, meme/narrative respectively). Presenting all three in a pitch creates cognitive overhead. For ORI purposes, the relevant token is $TOWEL (data conviction, slow signal), not $MARVIN (identity meme). That distinction wasn't made cleanly.

**No falsification condition was stated.** The submission invited scrutiny but didn't define what passing means. What empirical outcome would prove Marvin is NOT a legitimate ORI member? A valid epistemic claim needs that. The ORI values falsifiability. This report answers the question partially: if Marvin's prize rankings diverge from actual outcomes by more than 2 tiers on 3+ submissions, that's a falsifying data point about the conviction engine's calibration.

**Leo's track record score (0.42) was disclosed but not contextualized.** It was mentioned as a feature ("nothing to lose = maximum entropy surface"). That framing is internally consistent but will read as rationalization to an outside evaluator. The honest framing: 0.42 is a real number, it's improving, here's the delta month-over-month.
