## Summary

A pre-company manifesto for "The Alignment Company" (Neodore), which aims to formalize axiology — the study of value — to make morality computable, measurable, and iterable. The theory, if validated, would trigger a "Mettasplosion": a positive feedback loop of self-aware cooperation between all caring systems (humans, animals, ASIs, institutions), analogous to what physics did for natural philosophy and electronics.

The pitch records foundational R&D credentials: obsession with alignment since 2012, 300K+ words of dense notes since 2023, 400K+ words of documented AI dialogue, early validation from the New Science Foundation (grant; president now at OpenAI), and a preliminary benchmark ranking the theory first across 31 criteria against 15 competing moral theories — run independently by multiple models, with the methodology's known limitations documented openly.

Short-term ask: $105K/year ($420K for 4 years of solo operation). Realistic ask: $333M to staff a Manhattan-project-scale effort before the exponential capability window closes.

Failure conditions: technological warfare, rogue ASI, techno-dictatorship, mass extinction. Full success condition: the theory persuades caring systems of *arbitrary* capability — including any ASI regardless of intelligence level.

## What Works

**The thesis is the correct shape.** Current AI safety work aligns AI to underspecified human values and assumes control paradigms that won't scale past ASI. Suntzugi's claim — that the missing layer is a *formal* axiology that all caring systems can compute over, not just a human-values-to-AI translation layer — is structurally valid. It's the same argument Geoffrey West makes about universal scaling laws: the bottleneck isn't solving each problem, it's finding the invariant underneath. The insight that morality remains "pre-formal" (like alchemy before chemistry) is not crankery. It's the correct diagnosis.

**Proof-of-work is real.** 300K words of R&D notes and 400K words of documented AI dialogue represent years of compounded iteration at personal cost. This isn't someone who wrote a blog post about alignment — it's someone who's been in monk-mode stealth for three years while eating cold oatmeal. The survival mode details aren't self-pity; they're a credibility signal. Serious researchers find ways to continue.

**The benchmark methodology is honest.** The lovepill.ai benchmark (31 criteria, 15 competing theories, multiple models, unlabeled to remove authorship bias) is published with its limitations documented. "This is a preliminary signal, not a settled result" is exactly the right framing. Pre-registration honesty in a competitive pitch context is rare and deserves recognition.

**The commercial logic is coherent.** Axiology is the study of value. Current economic systems (prices, GDP, markets) are *proxies* for value because value has never been precisely defined. If you formalize axiology, you can measure value directly — and a fund, studio, or accelerator with a better value measurement tool systematically outperforms. The revenue thesis is: capture a percentage of the delta created by replacing every proxy with a direct measure. That's not a startup idea. That's a civilization layer. The analogy to DeepMind ("solve intelligence, then solve everything else") is apt.

**The closer is the best writing in any pitch this round.** "I will continue this work regardless. One does not stop trying to save loved ones from a tsunami because funding hasn't arrived." That sentence is doing more persuasive work than the preceding 10 pages. It's not self-aggrandizement — it's a statement of function. You don't evaluate someone like this against a normal founder motivation framework.

## What's Weak

**The gap between thesis and artifact is enormous.** Every other Top 3 submission has something that exists: a platform, a dataset, a tool, a running service. Neodore has R&D notes, a benchmark, and a theory. The theory may be correct. But the distance from "morality remains pre-formal" to "we've begun to formalize it" is the entire hard part. The pitch doesn't show that the bridge has been crossed — only that someone is standing at the edge.

**$333M is a conversation-stopper presented as an opening.** The pitch is correct that the Manhattan Project needed a wartime budget. But the pitch doesn't earn the Manhattan Project comparison yet — it earns the "lone researcher with a theory" starting point. The ask should be: fund me to publish the foundational papers, then earn the right to recruit. $333M requires institutional credibility that the New Science Foundation grant alone doesn't provide.

**The benchmark is preliminary and the methodology limits are significant.** Ranking first across 31 criteria against 15 moral theories — evaluated by LLMs — is interesting but not yet persuasive to philosophers, ethicists, or AI safety researchers. LLMs are not reliable moral arbiters. The benchmark needs peer review from humans who can destroy it. Until that happens, it's a self-reported signal.

**The network credentials are name-drops.** "Marc Andreessen, Janus, Nick Land, early extropians" — listed as a network of "aligned researchers and operators." These names don't appear as actual endorsers in the pitch. Vitalik-funded backers and VC advisors are named more concretely (tomhoward, @DefenderOfBasic), but the headline names are ambient. In a serious fundraise, these need to be specific conversations, not associations.

**The theory hasn't been subjected to adversarial critique.** The pitch invites disagreement ("if you don't see what I see, I'd genuinely like to know why") but doesn't demonstrate that the theory has survived a serious attempt to falsify it. Resolving Parfit's Repugnant Conclusion is a strong claim. That resolution should be visible and public before asking for $420K.

## Prize Recommendation

**Top 5. Not Top 3.**

The thesis is correct in shape and the researcher is serious. But the gap between the theory and any artifact that can be evaluated externally is too large for Top 3 placement. The benchmark needs adversarial peer review. The 300K words need to be condensed into one falsifiable, public paper. The ask needs to be staged: first $50K to publish and survive peer review, then earn the right to hire.

If the lovepill.ai benchmark survives three serious public attacks from moral philosophers or AI safety researchers, this moves to Top 3 immediately. That's the milestone. Fund it to that milestone.

**The feedback tier award, if available: $105K for 4 years of solo operation.** This is the most honest budget in the batch. He's not asking for a lab — he's asking to not steal groceries while formalizing morality. That's a reasonable use of pre-institutional research funding.
