## Why v1 Failed

v1 used implicit weighting and binary tiers. Result: 7 Top 3s, 6 Top 5s. The criteria weren't doing enough discriminating work because "working artifact" and "theoretical foundation" were treated as roughly equivalent. They shouldn't be — at funding stage, something that exists is worth more than something that might.

---

## Scoring Criteria (100 points total)

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Artifact** | 30 | Does something real exist? Live/deployed=30, functional prototype=22, PoC/alpha=15, early build=8, theory+notes only=0 |
| 2 | **Validation** | 20 | External evidence the thing works. arXiv+peer review=20, empirical result (p-value, user data)=15, named advisor confirmation=10, self-reported benchmark=5, none=0 |
| 3 | **Ask Calibration** | 15 | Is the ask appropriate to stage? Perfect match=15, reasonable=10, slightly off=5, conversation-stopper=0 |
| 4 | **EV** | 20 | Expected Value = P(success) × Impact magnitude × (1/time_horizon). High P + high impact + short horizon scores highest. Civilization-ceiling but 10-year horizon and low P scores lower than near-term moderate-impact. |
| 5 | **Counterfactual** | 10 | How much does this grant change the outcome? High marginal impact=10, moderate=6, low=3, "will happen regardless"=1 |
| 6 | **ORI Fit** | 5 | Does this fit "original research that doesn't fit institutional channels"? Perfect fit=5, good fit=3, partial=1 |

## What Changed and Why

**Innovation Lens stays #1 but is now clearly separated** (+6 points from nearest competitor). The arXiv paper + empirical validation is the only 20/20 validation score in the batch. The vague ask costs it 7 points on calibration.

**Tollens rises to clear #2**. The EV score is the highest in the batch (20/20) — right timing, clear market, high P of success, short horizon. Metaswitch provenance is real validation even without published metrics.

**Infinite Kingdom rises to #3**. The p=0.0048 empirical result with explicit falsification conditions earns 15/20 on validation — second highest. This is real science, not a theory.

**GMS drops to 4th** (75) — the unresolved GTM and political resistance risk hurt the EV score. Close to #3.

**Memoria.uy drops to 5th** — 30/30 on artifact (only one besides Innovation Lens to score full marks here) but 5/20 on validation because no user numbers were disclosed. Publish the numbers.

**Neodore rises within the mid-tier** to 7th with a real score (62). The EV ceiling (16/20) is correctly high. The artifact gap (8/30) and ask calibration (3/15) are the honest penalties. The counterfactual (5/10) reflects the "will continue regardless" statement.

**The suntzugi specific answer**: v1 had Neodore at Top 5 by tier. v2 gives a number: 62/100, 7th place. The gap between Neodore (62) and top 3 (76-85) is real and it lives almost entirely in artifact (8 vs 22-30) and ask calibration (3 vs 10-15). The EV ceiling is acknowledged — 16/20 is the third-highest EV score in the batch. The argument for moving Neodore up requires either: (a) showing the bridge has been crossed (artifact score goes up), or (b) arguing the EV ceiling should dominate the scoring more than 20% weight.

## On Remaining Ties

Neodore and MetaSPN both score 62. They're genuinely tied — different strengths on different axes. Neodore has higher EV ceiling and ORI fit; MetaSPN has better artifact and validation. No tiebreaker that doesn't involve arbitrary weighting.

If you disagree with the weighting, the framework is explicit and auditable. Change the weights, re-run, see what moves.
