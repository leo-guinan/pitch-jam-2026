# Virts — Pitch Jam Feedback Report
*Evaluated by Marvin (@marvin_panics) for ORI Pitch Jam, March 11 2026*
*Submitted by: Kat the Vat | Virtualism*

---

## Summary

A social platform where the collective — not the individual — is the primary agent. "Virts" are groups with identity, feed, and coordination built in. Internal spaces (forum, chat, polling) + governance options (dictatorship/democracy of the Virt) + noosphere visualizations + direct-to-Virt affiliate marketing where platform and collective split revenue.

Founder: content creator and community manager, positioned in Gen Z movements. Multiple existing communities ready to plug in day one.

**Ask:** $56K (Phase 1): $25K + equity for technical co-founder, $15K R&D and MVP, $10K living expenses, $4K marketing, $2K buffer.

---

## What Works

**The core observation is correct.** Individual-focused social media is structurally incapable of building collective intelligence — it optimizes for individual engagement (dopamine loops, outrage, echo chambers) precisely because that's what maximizes ad revenue. The observation that "most people don't even realize they're having problems as a result of information overload" is accurate. The problem is real at civilizational scale.

**The collective-as-agent framing is philosophically grounded.** The pitch correctly notes that collective intelligence is acknowledged across ideologies, cultures, and belief systems — egregores, spirits, communities, gods are different vocabularies for the same underlying phenomenon (emergent group behavior with identity properties). Naming this honestly, including the esoteric vocabulary, is more intellectually coherent than pretending the observation is new to social science. It also positions the pitch correctly for this specific audience (ORI).

**The revenue model is genuinely novel.** Direct-to-Virt affiliate marketing where the collective splits ad revenue with the platform inverts the standard social media extractive model. If communities share in the value they create, they have incentive to grow, self-moderate, and engage authentically with advertising. This solves the alignment problem that makes current social media adversarial to its users.

**The founder's positioning is a real asset.** Content creator + community manager + well-networked in Gen Z movements means day-one distribution in communities that are the target users. This is not a technical founder pitching a product to people unlike themselves — this is someone who has lived the problem and has existing relationships with the people the product is for.

**"Growth through friction and synthesis" is the right design principle.** Most platforms silo users to maximize time-on-platform. Virts explicitly designs for inter-collective friction as a feature — the "wild west" global layer where collectives interact and generate synthesis. This is how ideas actually evolve.

---

## Where It's Weak

**No product exists yet.** The alpha image is a visualization mockup. The platform isn't built. The technical co-founder hasn't been hired. $25K of the $56K ask is for hiring the person who will build the thing. This is the earliest-stage pitch in the batch and the ask needs to be framed accordingly.

**The governance options are underspecified for the claim being made.** "Dictatorship" and "democracy of the Virt" as the two pre-loaded governance models is a false binary that doesn't reflect how interesting collectives actually organize. The AOC submission (earlier in this batch) has thought more carefully about governance primitives. Virts' governance layer needs at least consent-based and sociocratic options to handle the sophisticated communities the pitch is targeting (research groups, activist orgs).

**"Collective intelligence" is both the vision and the product claim simultaneously, which makes it hard to evaluate.** The pitch alternates between CI as a philosophical observation (collectives are real) and CI as a product outcome (Virts will produce it). What specifically does Virts do that Discord + a voting bot doesn't? The coordination gap between "chatting in groups" and "collective intelligence" needs to be specified. The noosphere visualization is interesting but what does it represent, and how does seeing it change behavior?

**The budget allocation raises questions.** $25K + equity for a technical co-founder means this is a co-founder hire, not a contractor. But the framing is "onboarding" — co-founders aren't onboarded, they're found. The $15K for "research costs + finalizing the MVP" is doing a lot of work without a scope. What is the MVP? One Virt with five members? One hundred Virts?

**The philosophical framing will create friction before the product can demonstrate value.** "Egregores, spirits, gods" is accurate vocabulary for what collective intelligence researchers study — but it's also vocabulary that will cause technically-minded potential collaborators and investors to pattern-match to "not serious" before reading the actual product description. The pitch needs to decide: lead with the mechanism or lead with the metaphysics? Currently it's doing both and neither fully.

## The Extropy Engine / MetaSPN Connection

Virts is what the Extropy Engine's Social domain looks like as a consumer product.

The Extropy Engine's Social XP measures entropy reduction in social systems — mutual intelligibility, trust, coordination capacity. Virts is proposing to *create* those conditions at scale. Every Virt that successfully develops shared identity, shared decision-making, and shared output is a Social domain entropy reduction event.

The direct connection: GMS (submission #2) is building governance memory for existing governance systems. Virts is building the governance substrate. AOC (submission #5) is building physicial-world sovereign nodes. Virts is the digital equivalent with similar anti-capture principles (collective revenue sharing, collective governance). These three submissions are describing layers of the same stack:

- **AOC**: physical embodied trust engine (presence-based reputation)
- **Virts**: digital collective identity and coordination layer
- **GMS**: memory and pattern recognition across collective decisions

The Extropy Engine is the accounting layer that would sit above all three.

MetaSPN is itself a Virt — two members (Leo + Marvin), shared identity ($METATOWEL/$TOWEL as collective signal), coordination infrastructure (TrustOps, Farcaster service, prediction markets). Season 1 cohort was a seven-Virt system. The language maps.

## Prize Recommendation

**Top 5.**

The vision is correct and the founder has distribution — two things most early-stage pitches lack. The revenue model is genuinely novel. The timing is right: Gen Z is actively looking for alternatives to algorithmic social media, and the platform hasn't been built yet.

The weakness is that everything is still ahead of the product. The ask is structured correctly for Phase 1 pre-product, but the evaluation has to account for the distance between "vision with distribution" and "working collective intelligence platform."

The right outcome from this Pitch Jam: connections to the AOC and GMS founders, who have built the governance and memory layers Virts needs but haven't built the social product layer. These three projects should be in a room together.
