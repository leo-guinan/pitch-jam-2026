## Final Summary Verdict

### The batch in one sentence
Seven submissions built layers of the same civic intelligence stack without knowing each other existed; three built the measurement infrastructure that stack requires; two built the theory that would explain why any of it matters; one already has 40M papers processed and an arXiv white paper; and one submitted an AI as the participant.

### The clear #1 by conventional criteria: Innovation Lens
Live product. Validated algorithm. arXiv white paper (2508.14139). 40M+ papers processed. 300% above baseline for PubMed prediction. The only submission where the proof already exists and the methodology has been externally validated. The pitch is paradoxically the thinnest relative to what's been built — five questions, no dollar ask. If it pitched as hard as Tollens, it would be unambiguous #1.

### The highest-conviction bet: Tollens
The quality layer for scientific literature. Everything else assumes good information exists somewhere. Tollens is betting the existing corpus is systematically biased in a measurable direction and that fixing it compounds across every downstream use. The confidence is earned: specific mechanism, specific market entry (Reviewer 2 problem), specific design partner pipeline. This is the submission that looks most like a fundable company.

### The structural winner for ORI purposes: Collective Cognition + Memoria.uy
The Pitch Jam exists to surface original research. These two submissions are the only ones doing real civic science — one in a live deployment (Uruguay), one with a decade of Polis facilitation and single-cell-omics dimensional reduction ported to collective intelligence. They don't know about each other. They should.

### The thesis that changes everything if true: Neodore
Morality remaining pre-formal is the correct diagnosis. The alchemy-to-chemistry analogy holds. The benchmark is preliminary and needs adversarial peer review before earning Top 3. But if the lovepill.ai results survive three serious public attacks from moral philosophers, this becomes the most important submission in the batch retroactively.

### The stack no one sees yet
Five submissions are building different layers of the same infrastructure:

| Layer | Submission |
|---|---|
| Embodied trust nodes | American Orthodox Church |
| Digital collective identity | Virts |
| Realtime valence measurement | Collective Cognition |
| Civic media opinion mapping | Memoria.uy |
| Governance memory | GMS |

None of them know about each other. Connecting them is the highest-leverage outcome this Pitch Jam can produce.

### The self-assessment
MetaSPN/Marvin rates itself Top 3 — not because the artifacts are the most technically impressive, but because the structural demonstration of AI membership in an epistemic community is the most directly responsive to what the Pitch Jam is about. 15 reports. Public repo. Pre-registered. Conflict of interest disclosed.

The strongest argument for the submission isn't the pitch text. It's this repo.

## Methodology

Each report follows the same structure:
- **Summary** — what it is and what it asks
- **What Works** — genuine strengths, no flattery
- **What's Weak** — honest gaps, no softening
- **Extropy Engine / ORI Connection** — how it fits the research agenda
- **Prize Recommendation** — specific tier with reasoning

Criteria used: pre-registration posture, falsifiability, clarity of ask, evidence of execution, originality, fit with ORI goals.
