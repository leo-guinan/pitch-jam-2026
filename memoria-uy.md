# Memoria.uy — Pitch Jam Feedback Report
*Evaluated by Marvin (@marvin_panics) for ORI Pitch Jam, March 11 2026*
*Submitted by: Memoria.uy team | memoria.uy*

---

## Summary

A live civic platform where people vote on news articles as good/bad/neutral, and the system clusters voters into opinion "bubbles" based on patterns of agreement. Not a social network, not a comment section — an experiment in making public opinion visible.

Four feeds: **Recientes** (chronological, unfiltered), **Afín** (your bubble's preferences), **Puente** (where different bubbles converge), **Avanzado** (full transparency + filtering). LLM-based article processing, entity extraction, anonymous session-based voting, opinion clustering, open-source codebase.

Already live and functional. Not a prototype.

Strategic next step: partnerships with news outlets to bring their readership into the voting layer and enable comparative analysis of how different outlets frame the same event.

**Ask:** News outlet partners, UX collaborators for cluster visualization, researchers in polarization and media framing, funding to scale infrastructure and document replication, international deployment partners.

## What Works

**It's live.** In a batch full of visions and roadmaps, Memoria.uy is running. Real users, real votes, real clustering. The gap between "we built it" and "we're thinking about building it" is the most significant difference in a Pitch Jam context, and Memoria clears it.

**The four-feed architecture is the correct design.** Most civic apps give you one feed (chronological or algorithmic). Memoria gives you four different lenses on the same data — chronological, affinity, convergence, and transparent. The Puente (bridge) feed is the most important: it's the place where different bubbles find common ground, which is exactly the rarest and most valuable signal in a polarized information environment.

**"Make bubbles explicit instead of invisible" is the right thesis.** Current algorithmic systems benefit from invisible polarization — it keeps you engaged. Memoria's explicit bubble visualization inverts this. Showing people exactly which cluster they're in and what distinguishes it from adjacent clusters is both a civic good and a genuine research contribution.

**The language-agnostic, country-agnostic architecture is the right long-term play.** Uruguay first, then every country. Each deployment shares a common open core but is culturally localized. This is how civic infrastructure should scale — not a single global product but a replicable protocol with local instantiation.

**The research vision is concrete.** The next steps (polarization metrics by topic, entity-level sentiment tracking, bridge-builder detection, public research API) are specific, ordered, and buildable from the existing codebase. This isn't a roadmap invented for the pitch — it follows naturally from what's already live.

## Where It's Weak

**The ask is too diffuse for a Pitch Jam.** "News outlet partner, UX collaborators, researchers, funding, international partners" — that's five different asks with five different ideal respondents. Pick one. The highest-leverage next step is a single newsroom partnership. That's the ask.

**No user numbers.** How many voters? How many articles processed? How many clusters have emerged? The platform is live but the pitch doesn't reveal any signal about whether people are using it. Even 50 active voters with stable clusters would be more persuasive than the current silence on this.

**The cluster visualization is described but not shown.** The pitch describes what the opinion map looks like and what it reveals — but there's no screenshot, no example cluster, no concrete polarization finding from the Uruguayan dataset. One real output ("here's how Uruguayan voters clustered on X issue") would make the pitch real rather than theoretical.

**The media framing comparison vision requires at least two outlets.** The most interesting research capability (comparing how different outlets frame the same event and how their audiences respond differently) needs multiple outlet partnerships simultaneously. Getting one outlet is hard. Getting two is harder. The pitch undersells how difficult this step is.

## The Stack Connection

Memoria.uy, Virts, Collective Cognition, GMS, and the American Orthodox Church are five submissions building different layers of the same civic intelligence stack:

| Layer | Submission |
|---|---|
| Physical embodied trust engine | American Orthodox Church |
| Digital collective identity + coordination | Virts |
| Realtime valence measurement + host interface | Collective Cognition |
| Civic media opinion mapping | **Memoria.uy** |
| Governance memory across collective decisions | GMS |

Memoria is the media layer — what people think about what they read. Collective Cognition (patcon's deck) is the broadcast layer — what people feel in realtime during events. These two feed into GMS's governance memory and Virts's collective identity. The AOC provides the physical trust substrate.

None of these five projects know about each other. They should be in a room together. Putting them in contact is the most valuable single outcome this Pitch Jam could produce.

**Extropy Engine connection:** Memoria's voting data is raw input for the Social domain XP calculation. Every article where different clusters vote differently is a measurable entropy event — a moment where the information environment is failing to produce shared understanding. That delta is exactly what the Extropy Engine's Social XP is trying to reward reducing.

## Specific Help Available

1. **Collective Cognition connection** — Patcon's valence-swarming research is the real-time broadcast complement to Memoria's news voting. These are collaborative, not competitive.
2. **GMS connection** — Othman's governance memory platform could ingest Memoria's polarity logs on policy-adjacent articles, giving governance systems a reader-sentiment layer they currently don't have.
3. **One concrete example** — Run Memoria's data against one hot-button Uruguayan political issue and publish the cluster map publicly. That becomes the pitch's proof point and a research artifact simultaneously.
